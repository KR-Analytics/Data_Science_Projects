## Overview
This project presents an end-to-end analysis of FMCG (Fast-Moving Consumer Goods) sales data with the objective of identifying sales trends, regional performance, top-selling products, and customer segment contributions.

The workflow reflects a realistic analytics pipeline where **Excel** is used for data cleaning and analysis, followed by **Tableau** for interactive data visualization.

---

## Tools Used
- **Excel**
  - Data cleaning and formatting
  - Calculated fields
  - PivotTable-based analysis
- **Tableau**
  - Interactive dashboard development
  - Trend, comparison, and composition visualizations
  - Dashboard-level filters for exploration

---

## Dataset
The dataset consists of FMCG sales transactions with the following attributes:
- Order ID
- Order Date
- Product
- Category
- Region
- Customer Segment
- Quantity
- Unit Price
- Sales

Data preparation and validation were performed in Excel before visualization in Tableau.

---

## Analysis Performed
The following analyses were conducted as part of this project:

- **Monthly Sales Trend**  
  Month-level sales analysis to observe short-term performance changes within a single year.

- **Sales by Region**  
  Comparison of total sales across regions to identify high- and low-performing regions.

- **Top Products by Sales**  
  Identification of top revenue-generating products to understand sales concentration.

- **Customer Segment Contribution**  
  Evaluation of how different customer segments contribute to overall sales.

---

## Tableau Dashboard
An interactive Tableau dashboard was created to visualize key business metrics, including:
- Monthly Sales Trend
- Sales by Region
- Top Products by Sales
- Customer Segment Share

### Interactive Filters
The dashboard allows dynamic filtering by:
- **Year**
- **Region**
- **Category**

These filters apply across all visualizations to enable exploratory analysis.

---

## Key Insights
- Sales exhibit a gradual decline across the observed months, indicating mild short-term seasonality.
- Certain regions contribute a higher share of total revenue compared to others.
- A small number of products account for a significant portion of overall sales.
- The Consumer segment is the primary contributor to total revenue.
